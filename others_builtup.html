INTRODUCTION

Urbanization is a significant social and economic phenomenon which at present is taking place at an unprecedented scale and rate worldwide (C. Sun, Z. Wu, Z. Lv, N. Yao, and J. We, 2013). According to the Census 2011 by the Central Bureau of Statistics, Nepal, the urban population of Nepal increased from 2.9 percent in 1952/1954 to 17 percent in 2011. Built-up area expansion in Nepal, especially in case of Kathmandu district has been expanding in rapid amount due to major changes caused by anthropogenic and natural factors and their impacts on the national and regional environment and climate. The unprecedented urbanization accompanied by major changes in social, economic, environmental and technological arena are imposing impact on traditional land-use system, water resources and environmental quality. As a consequence, there is qualitative change in the urban ecology of the densely populated cities ( S.Ghosh and G.Giyasuddin, 2018).

OBJECTIVE

The principal objective of this project is to analyze the changes occurred in the built up areas between 2000, 2010 and 2019 with application of BUI.
